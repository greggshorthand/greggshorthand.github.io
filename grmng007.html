36.  The PEND Blend

     The present participles of verbs should be written with the ENT blend.  P-END join without an angle.  The PEND blend is also used for CHEND.

37.  The DEF Blend

     D and F when joined are written without an angle, and the blend represents the syllables: TIV, DEF, DIV, DEV.

38.  The MEM Blends

     The syllables MEM and MEN are represented by M lengthened.  The vowel of the syllable is not confined to e, as the above examples show.
     In NEN, NEM, HIM, NOM, NUM, etc., the vowel is always written.

39. The DET and SES Blends

     The syllables DET, DED are expressed by D lengthened.  The two forms of S joined represent SES or SIS; after a circle it is not necessarily to write the first S as its presence is clear from the direction of the second S.

40.  The CON- and COM- Prefixes

     K represents the syllables COM, CON (KOM, KON) when this is followed by a consonant; when it is followed by a vowel it is represented by KM or KN as the case may be.

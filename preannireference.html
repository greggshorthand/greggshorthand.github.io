Pre-Anniversary Reference Books

Gregg Shorthand: A Light-Line Phonography for the Million (Fifth Edition)

This is the official 1916 and 1919 Manual, demonstrating all the principles of the system with a few reading and writing exercises.

Gregg Shorthand Dictionary

This book contains a great many outlines according to the 1916 edition. This PDF is text-searchable for fast reference.

Gregg Speed Studies (Google)

This is a great textbook for speed practice and penmanship drills. It is hosted on Google Books.

Word and Sentence Drills for Gregg Shorthand (Google)

This useful book of drills for practicing theory concepts is hosted on Google Books.

Graded Readings in Gregg Shorthand (Google)

These are readings in Gregg Shorthand that adhere to the different units of the 1916 Manual.

Notes on Lessons in Gregg Shorthand (Google)

This is a useful book that gives extra advice and information on the concepts introduced in each chapter of the 1916 Manual.

Basic Principles of Gregg Shorthand

This is a very long treatise on the reasonings behind the principles of Gregg Shorthand, mostly in asserting its supposed superiority over Pitmanic shorthands. Retypeset by Andrew Owen.

Q's and A's of Shorthand Theory

This is an invaluable book for anyone seeking to understand some of the most frequent questions that were asked of J. R. Gregg about the system, and some of the least frequent.

Groceries and Foodstuffs

A collection of useful outlines for common grocery items. This is useful for making shopping lists.

Speed Pointers

A few bits of advice on gaining speed in shorthand.

Charles Lee Swem on Numbers

A few bits of advice on writing numerals in while writing shorthand.

Charles Lee Swem on Shortcuts

A few remarks by Swem on how to make brief outlines for commonly used words in one's field.

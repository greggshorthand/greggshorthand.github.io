Anniversary Reference Books

Gregg Shorthand: A Light-Line Phonography for the Million (Anniversary Edition)

This is the official Anniversary Manual, demonstrating all the principles of the system with a few reading and writing exercises.

Gregg Shorthand Dictionary

This book contains about 19,000 outlines, making it very useful to the shorthand writer. This PDF is text-searchable for fast reference.

Gregg Shorthand Phrase Book

This is the Anniversary version of a book of a considerable number of phrases, demonstrating many useful features of phraseology.

5,000 Most-Used Shorthand Forms

This 92-page book works a bit like a small dictionary of Gregg Shorthand Anniversary Series outlines. It can be a very helpful reference to the shorthand writer.  The outlines were compiled by Ernest Horn in the order of the presentation of the words' principles in the manual.

Fundamental Drills in Gregg Shorthand

Assembled by Gertrude Beers and Letha P. Scott and written by Mrs. Richmond, this book contains helpful drills in developing shorthand speed.  It is written with a different, but very beautiful and regular style by Winifred K. Richmond, who wrote the plates to the Anniversary manual and dictionary. 

Twenty Shortcuts to Shorthand Speed

A book that gives twenty basic pieces of advice on how to get faster at shorthand. It also has good perspectives on teaching the system.

Basic Principles of Gregg Shorthand

This is a very long treatise on the reasonings behind the principles of Gregg Shorthand, mostly in asserting its supposed superiority over Pitmanic shorthands. Retypeset by Andrew Owen.

Technical Vocabularies from the Gregg Writer

This is a collection of different technical vocabularies that were printed in the Gregg Writer.

Groceries and Foodstuffs

A collection of useful outlines for common grocery items. This is useful for making shopping lists.

Proper Names

Some of the most common surnames in the English-speaking world.  Very useful list with key in the back.

Speed Pointers

A few bits of advice on gaining speed in shorthand.

Charles Lee Swem on Numbers

A few bits of advice on writing numerals in while writing shorthand.

Charles Lee Swem on Shortcuts

A few remarks by Swem on how to make brief outlines for commonly used words in one's field.
